Why Me, Why Now

The Gap I See

Amperity's AI strategy needs someone who lives in both worlds

The Head of AI role calls for someone who can "translate complex ML capabilities into accessible, intuitive user experiences that drive adoption across technical and non-technical users." That's not an abstraction for me. It's what I do every day when I take Stitch output and make it explainable to a VP of Marketing at Macy's, or when I build a confidence-scoring algorithm that a GM regional lead can actually act on.

I also have a clear vision for where this goes: Amperity as an agentic platform where customers interact with their data through AI, not around it. The tools I've built — MCP integrations that query tenants conversationally, identity engines that investigate clusters autonomously, an AI tool-builder that generates its own new tools — are early prototypes of what that looks like. The next step is making these capabilities client-facing, not just internal. That's a product strategy, not a feature request.

What I Built

An AI-native operating system for enterprise customer success

I've built dozens of AI-powered tools — many for Amperity (identity explainability engines, intelligence dashboards, account strategy frameworks, agentic workflows), many on my own time (multi-model creative studios, multi-agent orchestration platforms, agentic marketing prototypes, realtime voice interfaces). The Amperity work is grounded in customer need. The personal work is where I push into what's next. Both feed the same vision.

My Thesis

The agentic future of customer data

From queries to conversations

Today, getting value from Amperity requires SQL fluency or waiting on a team with it. I've prototyped MCP integrations where users query tenants, investigate identity clusters, and build segments through natural conversation. Internally, this accelerates our teams. But the bigger move is making this client-facing — giving customers direct conversational access to their data through Amperity's platform. That changes the value proposition entirely.

From opaque to explainable

Stitch is powerful but hard to trust when you can't see why records merged. My identity explainability prototypes give customers confidence scores, merge reasoning, and drift monitoring. Explainability isn't a nice-to-have — it's the unlock for deeper enterprise adoption and the foundation for customers to self-serve their identity layer with confidence.

From manual to autonomous

One of my tools is a meta-tool that builds its own new tools. That pattern — AI that extends its own capabilities — is the trajectory. Data quality monitoring that flags and resolves issues before a human notices. Campaign optimization that learns from activation outcomes. Segment discovery that surfaces audiences no one thought to look for. The future isn't AI features bolted onto a CDP — it's a CDP that thinks alongside the marketer.

The full vision

Amperity becomes the platform where customer data isn't just stored and queried — it's understood, acted on, and continuously optimized by AI agents working in partnership with human teams. Client-facing agentic access via MCP. Identity resolution that explains itself. Predictive models that adapt in real time. Activation workflows that close the loop autonomously. Every piece of this exists in prototype form in the tools I've already built. The Head of AI role is where these prototypes become product strategy.
