⚠ The Cost of Inaction
Inflated customer counts across channels. Every audience size built on fragments not real shoppers. Campaign reach metrics, engagement rates, and customer counts are wrong by default — decisions made on these numbers compound the error.

Invisible high-value shoppers fragmented across brands. Best customers fragmented across Seahawks and Bloomingdale's look disengaged. A customer active across online, in-store, and loyalty appears as multiple low-value contacts — no retention outreach, no VIP treatment.
Loyalty program leakage. Star Rewards members duplicated across systems. Points and tier status can't be accurately consolidated. The loyalty program that should drive retention instead creates frustration when members aren't recognized across channels.
Cross-channel attribution impossible without unified identity. Did the email campaign drive in-store visits? Did the in-store experience increase online purchases? Without unified identity, attribution is guesswork and every marketing investment is harder to justify.
