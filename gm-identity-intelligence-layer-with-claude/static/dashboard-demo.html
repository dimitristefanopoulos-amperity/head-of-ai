02 — Identity Explainability

The why behind every merge

Amperity's native UI shows what merged. This engine explains why — with plain-language narratives, 4-component confidence scoring, pairwise analysis, and variation detection. Something no one else at Amperity has built.
Unique Capability

Confidence Scoring

4-component composite (0-100): signal strength, pairwise match quality, data consistency, and cross-source corroboration. With automatic penalties for overclustering and transitive-heavy linkage.

Unique Capability

Merge Narrative

Auto-generated plain-language explanation of why records resolved to one customer — shared PII anchors, variation patterns, source overlap, and score distributions. Auditable and client-presentable.

Platform Extension

Name Variant Detection

Surfaces customers that only Amperity's probabilistic matching can find — transliterations, maiden/married names, typos — invisible to deterministic approaches.

Platform Extension

Drift Monitoring

SQLite-backed persistence tracks identity resolution metrics over time. Automated alerting when dedup rates, cluster sizes, or source distributions shift beyond thresholds.

07 — What This Proves

Amperity's output is extensible

This engine is a proof of concept that Amperity's identity resolution output can be made transparent, auditable, and client-presentable — extending the platform's value rather than replacing any part of it.
The platform works. GM's three regions — different data volumes, different languages, different source systems — all resolved into unified customer identities. The numbers are real and they hold up under inspection.
Explainability creates trust. When you can show a client exactly why two records merged — with a confidence score, a narrative, and the actual pairwise scores — the conversation shifts from "is the data right?" to "what do we do with it?"
The COTM framing sells itself. Same data, different language. The Value View takes raw identity resolution metrics and tells a business outcome story that an executive can absorb without understanding what Stitch does.
Live data beats static slides. Everything in this demo is queried from production tenants in real time. The numbers change when the data changes. That credibility is impossible to replicate with screenshots in a PowerPoint.
